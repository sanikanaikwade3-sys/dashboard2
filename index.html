Smart City Air Quality Monitoring System

This dashboard is designed as a professional smart-city monitoring report for urban air-quality assessment. It combines AQI-based performance indicators, time-series analysis, pollutant behavior, location comparison, risk-category distribution, and environmental alerts in one unified dark-theme interface. The layout is intentionally large and information-rich so it resembles an industry-level command center rather than a simple classroom summary. AQI acts as a standardized indicator that converts complex pollutant concentration data into an understandable scale showing whether ambient air is safe, stressed, or hazardous for public exposure.

Dashboard Purpose

This report-style interface is suitable for assignment presentation, portfolio demonstration, and smart-city monitoring concepts. It is intentionally divided into multiple full sections so each analytical layer appears complete and professionally documented.

AQI categories are commonly represented with color-coded risk bands, where better air quality is shown in green and more dangerous conditions move toward orange, red, and darker high-risk tones. This dashboard adopts that visual convention for immediate interpretation and alert visibility.

      Effect of Key Pollutants

      • PM2.5: Very fine particles can enter deep respiratory pathways and are often considered among the most dangerous urban air pollutants.
      • PM10: Coarser particulate pollution is commonly associated with dust, construction activities, and road emissions.
      • NO2: Nitrogen dioxide is linked to combustion and traffic emissions and contributes to respiratory irritation and urban haze.
      • CO: Carbon monoxide interferes with oxygen transport in blood and becomes especially harmful under poorly ventilated, combustion-heavy conditions.
      • SO2: Sulfur dioxide is often associated with industrial emissions and can aggravate breathing discomfort and atmospheric degradation.
